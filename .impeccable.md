# Design Context

> Editorial-archive redesign of the Africa Multiple / University of Bayreuth
> reveal.js deck theme. Established 2026-06-16 via `/impeccable`.

## Users
Academic audiences at conferences, seminars and lectures — digital humanities,
African and Islamic studies, history. Presenter: Frédérick Madore, a historian
of Islam in francophone West Africa who works primarily with newspapers and
Islamic print. Decks are projected in lecture halls and published as a
self-contained, offline-capable site (`slides.frederickmadore.com`).

## Brand personality
Scholarly, archival, exacting — a printed monograph or humanities journal, not
a SaaS pitch deck. Three words: archival, considered, print-rooted. It should
feel authored by a historian, and never read as "AI-generated".

## Aesthetic direction — "editorial archive"
Ink on warm ivory paper. A book serif (EB Garamond) for prose and display; a
newspaper gothic (Libre Franklin) for the label/chrome voice — running heads,
eyebrows, folios, footer. Devices borrowed from print: running heads, ruled
figure-ledgers, hairline rules, hanging em-dash lists, marginal notes. The dark
"half-title" pages (section dividers, closing) use a flat deep field with a gold
plate rule.

**Anti-references (removed as generic tells):** the 6-colour rainbow spectrum
bar; the compass motif; tinted "key-takeaway" boxes; a green eyebrow + green
tick-rule on every slide; drop-shadowed rounded cards.

## Colour
The exact University of Bayreuth corporate palette is retained — green #009260,
navy #00268a, gold #cca352, amber #f59c08, brown #d57912, sky #44b8f2 — but
spent sparingly (60-30-10). Green is reserved for links, the occasional
emphasis, and small folio/section marks; gold accents the dark pages. Neutrals
are a warm-ivory paper / cool-navy ink pairing.

## Design principles
1. Type and rules carry the design — not boxes, shadows, or decorative graphics.
2. Colour is rare by default; an accent works *because* it is scarce.
3. Vary the page; never let identical furniture repeat on every slide.
4. Keep the Bayreuth identity through the palette + wordmark, not chrome gimmicks.
5. Everything self-hosted and offline-capable (fonts vendored via
   `shared/fonts/fetch-fonts.py`; re-run it to change the type choices).
